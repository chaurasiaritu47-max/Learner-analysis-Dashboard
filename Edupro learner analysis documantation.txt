Chapter 1: Project Overview
1.1 Project Title
“Learner Demographics and Course Enrollment Behavior Analysis on EduPro”.
1.2. Introduction
* Analysing Edupro online learning platform data facilitates comprehension of demographic patterns, course popularity, and learner behaviour. In order to find trends in age groups, gender distribution, course categories, and course levels, this study examines learner enrolment data. 
* The intention is to offer information that enhances educational planning and decision-making. This project creates an interactive dashboard with important metrics and visuals using Python and Streamlit.

1.3. Objective
The main objectives of this project are:
* To analyze learner enrollment data
* To identify popular courses
* To understand which age group is more involved in this learning platform
* To analyze gender participation
* To analyze learner pattern by creating visual dashboard

1.4 Stakeholder
* In order to understand enrollment trends and learner behaviour, this research uses data from education platforms to study student demographics and course preferences. The data reveals that course enrolments varied between genders and age groups, demonstrating that residents have a variety of learning demands. 
* Strong interest in employability-focused education is indicated by the higher enrollment in some course categories, such as technical and professional skills. The value of fundamental learning programs is highlighted by the large enrolment in beginner-level courses.
* The interactive dashboard helps stakeholders successfully track trends in education by offering real-time information about enrolments by age, gender, category, and course level. Better resource allocation and training program planning may be facilitated by these findings. 
* The results can support inclusive education activities and assist in identifying underserved student groups. Finding high-demand skill areas for workforce development can also be aided by the dashboard. All things considered, this project facilitates data-driven decision-making.

Chapter 2: Data Description
2.1. Dataset Description

The dataset contains information about the learner and their enrollments. There are 4 sheets in the file for student, Teacher, Course and Transaction with many columns but the important column which I have used are as follow:
Column NameDescriptionUserIDUnique identification number assigned to each learner.TeacherIDUnique identification number assigned to each Teacher.CourseIDUnique identification number assigned to each Course.TransactionIDUnique identification number assigned to each Transaction. This is the main column used to join the tables.AgeAge of learners.GenderGender of learners.CourseNameName of the course learner enrolled in.CourseCategoryCategory or field of the course (e.g., Data Science, AI, Web Development).CourseTypeType of course such as Free or PaidCourseLevelDifficulty level of courses (Beginner, Intermediate, Advance)  
2.2. Data Pre-processing
* Merging Data:
* In this Edupro online platform data there are 4 sheets such as User for student data, Teacher, Course and Transaction. For analysing and creation of dashboard this sheet needs to be combined.
* Each Sheet having unique Id to recognize each record uniquely. Transaction sheet is having all the unique ID of sheet like UserID, TeacherID, CourseID and TransactionID so This TransactionID column is used to merge all the sheets.
* Handling Missing values:
* To enhance data quality, missing values were found and eliminated from the dataset. Direct removing the missing value row is not a correct way to handle them as it increase loss of data.
instead of remove the missing value we can replace the missing value with the Arithmetic value (Mean,Median,Mode) Errors during analysis and visualization were avoided as a result .
* Cases are different to use the Arithematic values. If the column values are numerical we can replace missing values with mean or median and with mode for categorical values.

Chapter 3: Methodology

3.1. Tools and Technologies used
Excel : Excel is used to save the csv file after pre-processing.

Chapter 4: Visualization Results

4.1. Key Findings

* Young Learner Dominate
* According to the age distribution data, most students are in the 20–30 age range. This suggests that the majority of users of online learning systems are young adults. 
* The majority of people in this age group are either students or professionals in their early careers who want to advance their careers. 
* Older age groups' lower participation rates raise the possibility that they lack the time or inclination to sign up for online courses. 
* This observation emphasizes how younger audiences that are interested in career development find online learning platforms more appealing. 
* By focusing on this demographic, course enrollment and engagement can rise dramatically.

* Technology Courses are Popular
* Technical courses like Data Science, Artificial Intelligence, and Machine Learning, Wed Development had the largest enrollments, according to the course category study. 
* These courses are well-liked because they provide excellent career prospects and are highly sought after in the labour market. 
* More and more students are selecting courses that help them develop useful, employable skills. Enrollments in non-technical courses are significantly lower, suggesting a trend toward education that emphasizes technology. 
* The growing significance of digital skills in contemporary industries is reflected in this trend. The platform can gain by offering more technical courses.

* Beginner course have high demand
* Beginner-level courses have substantially higher enrollment than intermediate and advanced courses, according to the data. This implies that many students are unfamiliar with the disciplines and would rather begin with fundamental ideas. 
* A wider audience can take beginner courses because they are frequently simpler to understand and require less prior knowledge. The decreased enrollment in advanced courses could be a sign that students become disinterested or find advanced subjects more difficult. 
* Creating organized learning pathways for students ranging from novice to expert could aid in their retention. The significance of beginner-friendly material on the site is demonstrated by this observation.

* Balanced gender participation
* Both male and female students actively participate in online courses, according to the gender analysis. Both genders are interested in different course categories and levels, according to enrollment trends. 
* There is no discernible gender difference in course enrollment, indicating equal access to educational options. This equilibrium shows that all people can access and use online learning tools. 
* Males and girls have similar learning styles, as evidenced by their preference for beginner-level courses. Sustaining gender inclusion can contribute to ongoing enrollment growth and diversity.

Chapter 6: Recommendation
6.1 Recommendation
* Increase Beginner level courses
* The data reveals that learners are most enrolled in beginner-level courses. It is clear from this that a large number of users are either new to online learning or are just learning the fundamentals. 
* To draw in new users, the platform should concentrate on introducing more courses that are suitable for beginners. There should be clear learning pathways available for both beginners and experts.This will help students maintain their interest and progressively advance their abilities. Overall enrollment can be greatly increased by adding more beginner-level content.

* Encourage Advance learning
* Enrollment in advanced courses is lower than that of beginner courses. This implies that students might not be carrying on with their education. From beginner to higher levels, the platform should offer defined learning pathways.
*  Rewards and certificates might encourage students to finish advanced courses. Giving advice and suggestions might also assist students in selecting their next course.
*  Promoting higher education will help users strengthen their skills.

* Target young Learners
* The majority of students are younger, particularly those in the 20–30 age range. Career advancement and skill improvement are very important to this group. Students and young professionals should be the target audience for marketing efforts. 
* More students may be drawn in by offering special discounts or student-friendly initiatives. Younger audiences might be reached by promoting courses on social media channels. By concentrating on this demographic, platform engagement can rise.

* Increase Course Recommendation
* Different course preferences are displayed by different age groups and genders. Learner demographics should inform the recommendation of a personalized course. Students will find it easier to locate pertinent courses as a result. 
* Recommendation systems can enhance user pleasure and experience. When classes align with students' interests, enrollment rates are higher. Enrollment rates can rise with better recommendations.
